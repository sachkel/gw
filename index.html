This comprehensive brand guide establishes the foundation for Gajawaja's identity as Maharashtra's premier digital Marathi infotainment portal. It provides detailed specifications and guidelines to ensure consistent, powerful brand expression across all touchpoints.

1.2 BRAND STORY & VISION

Our Story

Gajawaja emerged from a passionate vision to create a digital platform that celebrates and elevates Marathi language, culture, and current affairs in the modern media landscape. Founded in 2023, we began as a small team of journalists, content creators, and digital media specialists united by our love for Maharashtra's rich heritage and our commitment to delivering quality information and entertainment in Marathi.

Our name "Gajawaja" (गाजावाजा) – which evokes the idea of making an impact, creating a buzz, and celebrating with fanfare – reflects our mission to amplify Marathi voices and stories in the digital realm, bringing them to the forefront of contemporary discourse.

From our modest beginnings, we have steadily built a platform that bridges traditional Marathi content with modern digital presentation, offering our audience a unique blend of substantive news, cultural insights, and engaging entertainment – all in their mother tongue.

Today, Gajawaja stands as a distinctive voice in Maharashtra's digital landscape – respecting our linguistic and cultural heritage while embracing innovative approaches to content creation, design, and audience engagement.

Our Vision

To be Maharashtra's most trusted and engaging digital infotainment platform, recognized for authentic Marathi content that informs, entertains, and fosters cultural pride and connection.

We envision a future where:

  • Marathi language thrives and evolves in the digital space without losing its essential character
  • Quality information and entertainment in Marathi is easily accessible to all demographics
  • Marathi culture, achievements, and perspectives are celebrated and shared widely
  • Modern digital innovation enhances the expression and preservation of Marathi identity
  • Our platform serves as a virtual community center for Marathi speakers worldwide

2.2 LOGO USAGE & SPECIFICATIONS

Clear Space

To ensure visual impact, maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "गा" in "गाजावाजा." This space should be free from other graphic elements, text, or margins.

[CLEAR SPACE DIAGRAM PLACEHOLDER]

Minimum Size

To maintain legibility, observe these minimum size requirements:

  • Standard logo: 30mm (print) / 120px (digital) in width
  • Symbol only: 10mm (print) / 40px (digital) in height
  • Devanagari wordmark: 25mm (print) / 100px (digital) in width
  • Latin wordmark: 20mm (print) / 80px (digital) in width

[MINIMUM SIZE DIAGRAM PLACEHOLDER]

Logo Placement

The logo should ideally be placed in one of these positions:

  • Top left corner (primary position for Western reading direction)
  • Top right corner (alternative position)
  • Centered at top or bottom (for symmetrical layouts)
  • Bottom of content area (for scrolling digital content)

Digital-Specific Considerations

  • Responsiveness: Use appropriate logo versions for different screen sizes
  • Header Behavior: Consider fixed vs. scrolling header placement
  • Loading Sequence: Logo may appear before other content in loading screens
  • Touch Targets: Ensure logo links have sufficient touch area on mobile

Incorrect Usage

To protect our brand integrity, never:

  • Alter the logo colors outside of approved colorways
  • Stretch or distort the logo proportions
  • Add effects such as shadows, glows, or gradients
  • Rotate or angle the logo
  • Place the logo on low-contrast backgrounds
  • Enclose the logo in shapes unless using approved containers
  • Alter the relationship between the symbol and wordmark
  • Add unofficial taglines or text to the logo
  • Use the logo as a pattern or repeated element
  • Translate the logo into other scripts without approval

  • Tracking: +10
  • Leading: 130%

Line Length

  • Mobile: 35-45 characters
  • Long-form: Maximum 80 characters

Typography Guidelines

Script Mixing

  • Maintain clear visual hierarchy between Devanagari and Latin scripts
  • For bilingual text, balance visual weight through appropriate size adjustments
  • Default to Devanagari prominence in bilingual content
  • Separate scripts visually through spacing, color, or styling

Accessibility Considerations

  • Maintain minimum 16px base font size for body text
  • Ensure sufficient contrast (minimum 4.5:1 for body text)
  • Use relative units (rem/em) for responsive scaling
  • Consider readability needs for older audiences
  • Test typography across device types and screen sizes

Special Typography Treatments

  • Pull Quotes: Sahitya Serif Italic at 120-150% of body size
  • Statistics/Numbers: Waja Sans Medium with tabular figures
  • Breaking News: Gaja Marathi Bold with accent color
  • Cultural Terms: Subtle emphasis through Sahitya Serif

  • Use unnecessarily academic or technical language

3.4 WRITING STYLE GUIDE

Marathi Language Guidelines

Standard Usage

Gajawaja uses standard, widely-understood Marathi that balances linguistic purity with contemporary usage:

  • Follow standard Marathi grammatical conventions and sentence structure
  • Use widely understood vocabulary, avoiding overly sanskritized or anglicized extremes
  • When technical or English terms are necessary, provide Marathi explanations
  • Respect formal writing conventions while maintaining accessibility

Dialect Considerations

Maharashtra encompasses several Marathi dialects and regional variations:

  • Use standard Marathi for general content to ensure broad understanding
  • Incorporate regional dialect selectively for quoted speech or region-specific content
  • When featuring dialect, provide context for broader audiences
  • Treat all regional variations with equal respect and authenticity

Evolving Language

Marathi, like all languages, continues to evolve:

  • Balance respect for traditional Marathi with acceptance of natural language evolution
  • Incorporate new terminology appropriately, especially for technology and contemporary concepts
  • When creating or using new terms, ensure clarity through context or explanation
  • Document emerging vocabulary to ensure consistent usage across content

General Writing Principles

Clarity & Concision

  • Prioritize clear communication above all
  • Use active voice in most contexts
  • Keep sentences at moderate length (15-20 words average)
  • Break complex ideas into digestible paragraphs (3-5 sentences average)
  • Eliminate unnecessary words and repetition

Structure & Organization

  • Begin with the most important information (inverted pyramid for news)
  • Use descriptive, informative headlines and subheadings
  • Organize content with logical progression
  • Use bulleted or numbered lists for series of points
  • Include transition elements between sections

Digital Readability

  • Write scannable content with key information highlighted
  • Use shorter paragraphs for digital reading (1-3 sentences ideal)
  • Incorporate meaningful subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs
  • Format for mobile reading with even shorter paragraphs
  • Include visual breaks for longer content

Content-Specific Guidelines

News Writing

  • Begin with a clear, factual lead answering who, what, when, where, why, how
  • Attribute information to specific sources
  • Separate fact from opinion clearly
  • Provide context and background for complex topics
  • Use neutral language that avoids bias or sensationalism

Feature Writing

  • Begin with engaging, scene-setting introduction
  • Develop narrative flow with beginning, middle, and end
  • Include descriptive elements that bring stories to life
  • Incorporate human elements through quotes and perspectives
  • Conclude with meaning or connection to broader themes

Instructional Content

  • Present clear objectives at the beginning
  • Break processes into numbered steps
  • Use direct, action-oriented language
  • Anticipate and address common questions
  • Include visual supports when possible

Inclusive Language

Gender Inclusivity

  • Use gender-neutral terms when gender is not relevant
  • Present balanced representation of genders in examples and stories
  • Avoid stereotypical gender roles and assumptions
  • Be conscious of gendered language patterns in Marathi

Cultural Sensitivity

  • Represent Maharashtra's diverse communities respectfully
  • Avoid generalizations about any group or community
  • Consider how language might be interpreted by different audiences
  • Seek input from community members when covering specific groups

Age Inclusivity

  • Avoid language that stereotypes age groups
  • Consider needs of both younger and older readers
  • Balance traditional and contemporary terms and references
  • Present intergenerational perspectives when relevant
